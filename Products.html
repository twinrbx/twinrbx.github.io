I sell a variety of theatre-adjacent products on Roblox. All of my products besides TWINKET can be purchased in my Roblox product hub. For inquiries about TWINKET, please see TWINKET.

3.1 SystemsðŸ”—

  • Role Board System (1,299 Robux)

    • Advanced role board system allowing for easy organization of roles during a show. Actors can claim the role of the character they’re playing, and (at the game creator’s discretion) casting notices can be created that allow audience members to see who will be performing in what role. Additionally, role walls can be created, which prevent players who haven’t claimed a certain role from entering an area. This is especially helpful for restricting morphs from being used by the wrong players.

  • Autograph System (599 Robux)

    • Allow actors in your show to visually sign audience members’ playbills using an autograph pen. Signature positions and fonts are randomized so no two signed playbills are the same. Up to 7 different actors can sign one playbill. Incredibly easy to set up. A great way to create a unique, screenshottable moment for patrons after your show.

  • R15 Morph System (599 Robux)

    • Highly customizable, proximity-prompt based R15 morph system. Allows for easy creation of costumes which users can seamlessly equip and switch between.

  • Projection Screen System (75 Robux)

    • Efficient projection system that supports any amount of screens and any amount of projections. Usage can be whitelisted to certain players or allowed for everyone. Game creator and those with access to edit the game can also access projection logs in-game, which is helpful for preventing abuse of the system when everyone has access to it (for example, in tech games). Three different speed settings when projecting an image, each of which is customizable.

3.3 TWINKETðŸ”—

TWINKET is the theatre community’s premiere in-game ticketing system. It makes ticketing easy for both companies and patrons, allowing companies to entirely automate ticketing, and allowing patrons to easily reserve a specific seat for a specific performance date.
This demonstration is slightly outdated and will be updated soon.A product demonstration of TWINKET is available here.

3.3.1 Key FeaturesðŸ”—

  • Specific Seat Selection: Rather than requiring companies to prescribe seats as general admission (as other ticketing services do because of technical constraints), companies work with TWINKET to create a custom seat map for their venue. This allows patrons to precisely decide where they want to sit. Moreover, theatre owners can choose the price at which each individual seat is booked.

  • Multiple Event Support: Companies have the option to sell tickets for multiple different events, or for multiple different performances of a show, concurrently. By consequence, patrons can hold tickets to multiple events under the same company simultaneously. For example, a patron could book a seat for the opening performance of a show and the closing show, in the same or different seats, at their discretion.

  • Venue Ticket Utility (VTU): For an additional fee, companies can have TWINKET’s Venue Ticket Utility installed in their venue. This makes the usher process practically automatic. Patrons enter the venue with their ticket, represented as a tool, automatically in their inventory. Ushers are equipped with ticket scanning tools, which, upon scanning a ticket, allow the ticket holder to enter the theatre space. Ticket holders are only able to sit in the seat they reserved — other seats will not be accessible to them, making the process of seating patrons realistic yet trivial.

  • Seat Notices: TWINKET makes it easy to ensure patrons understand information particular to a specific seat or group of seats before booking. For example, you can mark certain seats as partial/obstructed view, or indicate that a seat is not an aisle seat (even if it looks like one on the seat map). These seat notices are noted prior to selecting the seat with either a distinct seat color or an exclamation point symbol on the seat. When the user clicks one of these seats, they will be prompted to read and confirm their understanding of a message of your choice.

  • Discord Logging: While the database of ticket purchases is hosted entirely on Roblox, TWINKET allows companies to easily set up a Discord channel where all new bookings will be logged. This makes it easy for company owners to track how well their event is selling on the go.

3.3.3 PricingðŸ”—

TWINKET is not open-source software; it is available for per-production licensing only.

When you license TWINKET for your production, you’ll pay a flat fee for the creation of your theatre’s ticketing portal. For each performance of the production you sell tickets for besides the first, you will be charged an additional small fee.

Pricing for TWINKET is unique to your theatre, and is determined based on a number of factors relating to that theatre; namely, its capacity, complexity, and curvature.

If you have used TWINKET before and are wanting to use it again in the same theatre, you are likely eligible for a discount. This is because a new seat map does not need to be created, which is the source of most of the labor when setting up TWINKET.

3.3.4 Accepted Payment MethodsðŸ”—

You can pay for TWINKET using either Robux or United States Dollars (USD).

Regarding Currency Conversions
The DevEx exchange rate for Robux is $350 per 100,000 robux, i.e., $0.0035 per 1 Robux. All Robux payment is done through T-shirt purchase, which Roblox keeps 30% of. Therefore, the USD equivalent of any Robux price is the Robux price times 0.00245. It is TWINKET’s policy to always round up to the nearest dollar, and to calculate conversions on a per-fee basis rather than for the total cost.

It should then be obvious that USD payment is highly preferable.
